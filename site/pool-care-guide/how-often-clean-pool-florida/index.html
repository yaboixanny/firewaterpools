How Often Should You Clean Your Pool in Florida?

Learn the ideal pool cleaning frequency for Florida's climate. Weekly vs. bi-weekly service, seasonal factors, and signs your pool needs attention.

One of the most common questions we get from new Florida homeowners is: "How often do I really need to clean my pool?" In many parts of the country, you might get away with bi-weekly service, but Florida is different.

The Florida Factor: Heat and Humidity

Florida's subtropical climate creates the perfect breeding ground for algae and bacteria. The combination of intense sunlight, high heat, and regular rainfall means your pool requires more frequent attention than pools in cooler climates.

Weekly Service is the Standard

For 99% of Florida residential pools, weekly cleaning is the bare minimum to maintain sanitary and safe swimming conditions. During a weekly visit, a professional will:

  • Test and balance chemicals (pH, chlorine, alkalinity, stabilizer)
  • Skim the surface for debris
  • Brush the walls and tile line
  • Empty skimmer and pump baskets
  • Check the filter pressure
  • Vacuum as needed

When You Might Need More

There are times when even weekly service isn't enough. If you have heavy screened enclosure debris, a lot of swimmers (like during summer break), or after a heavy storm, you might need to check your baskets and skimmer in between professional visits.

The Impact of Rain

Florida's afternoon thunderstorms can dilute your pool's chemicals and introduce phosphates and other contaminants. Consistent weekly service ensures that your water chemistry is corrected before algae has a chance to take hold.

Summary

In summary, stick to a weekly schedule for the best results. Consistency is key to preventing expensive cleanup jobs down the road.
